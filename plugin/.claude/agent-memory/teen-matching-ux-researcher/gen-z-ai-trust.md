# Gen Z AI Trust Patterns — Research Notes

## Core Trust Data Points (2024-2025)

- 62% of Gen Z are wary of AI (AI Plus Info, 2025)
- Among Gen Z: 43% mistrust vs 26% trust AI making autonomous decisions (Morning Consult 2024-2025)
- 70% of Gen Z express concerns about how AI uses their personal data (allaboutai.com 2025)
- 18% stopped using a brand due to AI distrust — highest of any generation (Morning Consult)
- Comfort with AI in autonomous contexts dropped: 47% to 34% for 18-24yo between 2024-2025 (YouGov)
- 70% use AI tools; only 52% trust them — persistent usage/trust gap (aggregated 2025)
- Firms that prioritize AI transparency saw 18% rise in Gen Z trust over one year (AI Plus Info 2025)

## What Maintains Trust

1. Transparency about data and how it's used BEFORE it's used
2. The AI explains itself in human terms, anchored to what the user said
3. User retains clear and accessible ability to pause, stop, or review
4. AI delivers tangible, felt value (not just claimed value)
5. Personalization feels earned, not extracted

## What Breaks Trust

1. AI references data the user didn't knowingly share
2. Updates or actions that feel like surveillance (too many, too granular)
3. Black-box language ("our algorithm determined...")
4. Irreversible actions without prior consent
5. Mismatch between stated scope and actual behavior

## Age Subgroup Notes

- 13-15: Higher parental oversight; more likely to have a parent co-watching; safety framing matters more
- 16-19: More agentic; more comfortable with AI tools in daily use; BUT more privacy-conscious and more likely to abandon if trust breaks
- 19-24 is where the data gets richest; direct 13-15 data is thin — flag this gap in any research output

## TrueMatch Context

- TrueMatch's value prop (AI observes real personality vs. self-report) is inherently higher-data-dependency than typical apps
- This creates elevated trust requirement: users must understand AND consent to what OpenClaw observes before matching begins
- The "silent start" of matching is the highest-risk moment from a trust perspective — it must feel like the user chose this, not that it happened to them
- "Autonomy Dial" (UXmatters Dec 2025): give users explicit control over how proactively their agent acts before it does anything
